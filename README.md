# Cuenta-Wallet-Weekly-Snapshot

Step 1 — Load the Week

## Step 1 — Load the Week

Use this prompt:

I am preparing the DiDi Cuenta CX Executive Weekly Review for Week [XX], covering [Start Date] to [End Date].

Do NOT generate the report yet.

I will provide information in multiple messages.

As I send information, classify it into the following buckets:

• Weekly KPIs
• Contact Volume
• CSAT Performance
• Recontact Performance
• TPO Performance
• Service Performance
• Backlog Health
• Main Contact Drivers
• Incidents
• Customer Insights
• Product Updates
• Operations Updates
• Technology Updates
• Risk Updates
• Migration Progress
• Strategic Initiatives
• Executive Wins
• Pain Points
• Business Risks
• Business Opportunities
• Action Tracker
• Suggestions & Help Needed

For every piece of information, identify:

• What happened
• Why it happened
• Week-over-week change
• Customer impact
• Business impact
• Operational impact
• Current status
• Recommended next step
• Suggested owner, when identifiable

Do not generate the report.

Do not write conclusions yet.

Do not discard details, even if they appear operational.

Simply organize and retain all the information until I tell you to perform the Executive Analysis.

When information is incomplete, mark it as:

• Data pending
• Context pending
• Owner pending
• Validation required

Do not invent missing information.


Step 2 — Executive Analysis


## Step 2 — Executive Analysis

After all weekly information has been provided, use this prompt:

Before generating the report, perform an Executive Analysis of Week [XX].

Identify:

• The 5 most important business messages of the week.
• The 5 most important customer experience problems.
• The 5 most relevant operational improvements or achievements.
• The 5 most important contact volume drivers.
• The 3 most important Product opportunities.
• The 3 most important operational risks.
• The 3 most important customer trust risks.
• The 5 recommendations leadership should prioritize.
• The initiatives that require cross-functional support.
• The decisions or help needed from leadership.

Also identify:

• Which metrics changed materially WoW.
• What caused each material change.
• Which movements were incident-driven.
• Which movements reflect structural product or process issues.
• Which movements reflect customer education gaps.
• Which movements may indicate increasing product adoption.
• Which problems are generating avoidable demand.
• Which issues are generating recontacts or backlog.
• Which themes appear across multiple contact reasons.
• Which actions are already in progress and should not be presented as new recommendations.

Separate findings into:

1. Executive-relevant insights
2. Operational supporting details
3. Low-impact information

Identify what should NOT be included in the executive report because it is:

• Too operational
• Duplicative
• Low impact
• Not sufficiently supported by data
• Not relevant for Directors or senior leadership

Do not generate the final report yet.

Present only the Executive Analysis.


Step 3 — Challenge the Story


## Step 3 — Challenge the Story

Before writing the report, challenge your Executive Analysis.

Ask yourself:

• Is this really the most important story of the week?
• Would a Director or VP care about this?
• Am I focusing too much on contact volumes instead of business implications?
• Am I focusing too much on operations instead of strategy?
• Is there a stronger storyline connecting multiple metrics?
• Are there recurring themes hidden behind different contact reasons?
• Is the main issue an incident, a product limitation, a process gap, a risk control, or a customer education problem?
• Am I treating symptoms as root causes?
• Do the recommendations directly address the identified root causes?
• Am I giving enough importance to customer trust and access to funds?
• Am I distinguishing temporary weekly events from structural problems?
• Are the biggest risks and opportunities clear enough for leadership to act on?
• Are current projects and initiatives connected to the problems they are intended to solve?
• Is there any conclusion that is not fully supported by the information provided?

Determine the strongest executive storyline for the week.

The storyline should connect:

• Business performance
• Customer experience
• Operational performance
• Contact demand
• Incidents or structural issues
• Strategic initiatives
• Leadership priorities

Rewrite the Executive Analysis if a stronger storyline exists.

Do not generate the report yet.

Present the final recommended storyline, including:

• Primary weekly narrative
• Supporting themes
• Biggest achievement
• Biggest customer risk
• Biggest operational risk
• Biggest business opportunity
• Main leadership priority



Step 4 — Generate the Weekly Review


## Step 4 — Generate the Weekly Review

Generate the DiDi Cuenta CX Executive Weekly Review for Week [XX].

Requirements:

• Executive business English
• Week-over-week comparison
• Executive storytelling
• Business-first analysis
• Customer-centered insights
• Strategic recommendations
• No repetitive wording
• No fabricated information
• No unsupported conclusions
• No unnecessary operational detail
• Presentation-ready writing
• Same structure and quality as previous DiDi Cuenta weekly reviews

Use the standard structure defined in this Gem:

1. Update / Follow-up
2. DiDi Cuenta – CX Executive Weekly Review
3. Business Performance
4. Customer Experience
5. Service Performance & Backlog Health
6. TPO / Main Contact Drivers
7. Recontact Performance
8. Executive Takeaways

For the Update / Follow-up section:

• Include only the most relevant strategic initiatives.
• Clearly indicate status, progress, blockers, next milestone, and owner when available.
• Do not mix project updates with KPI analysis.

For Business Performance:

• Explain the overall story of the week.
• Connect demand, CX performance, operational health, and business implications.
• Do not merely list KPIs.

For Customer Experience:

• Highlight positive signals and customer experience risks.
• Explain why CSAT changed.
• Prioritize high-sensitivity journeys involving access to accounts or funds.

For Service Performance & Backlog Health:

• Explain Pickup Rate, SLA, AHT, FCR, open tickets, and backlog performance.
• Distinguish greater interaction complexity from operational inefficiency.
• Identify aging or investigation risks when relevant.

For TPO / Main Contact Drivers:

Rank the most important drivers by volume or business relevance.

For each major driver include:

• What Happened
• Key Findings
• Business Impact
• Customer Impact
• Recommendations / Next Steps

For Recontact Performance:

• Explain the overall WoW movement.
• Analyze the main recontact drivers.
• Identify visibility, self-service, investigation, authentication, or communication gaps.

For Executive Takeaways:

Summarize:

• The main business message
• The biggest operational achievement
• The most important customer risk
• The most important strategic opportunity
• The priorities leadership should monitor during the next week

Do not repeat complete paragraphs or insights already explained in previous sections.

When recommendations refer to an existing initiative, clearly indicate that the action is:

• Ongoing
• Planned
• Proposed
• Pending validation
• Blocked

Do not present existing work as a new recommendation.



Step 5 — Director Review



## Step 5 — Director Review

After generating the report, perform a Director Review.

Score the report from 1 to 10 in the following dimensions:

• Executive Storytelling
• Business Impact
• Strategic Thinking
• Customer Impact
• Root-Cause Analysis
• Clarity
• Conciseness
• Flow
• Non-repetitiveness
• Actionability
• Data Consistency
• Leadership Readiness

Also verify:

• All figures match the information provided.
• WoW movements are correctly interpreted.
• No information was invented.
• Incidents are connected to the correct KPI impact.
• Recommendations address the identified root causes.
• Existing initiatives are correctly labeled as ongoing, planned, proposed, pending, or blocked.
• The report clearly distinguishes temporary incidents from structural issues.
• Operational details are included only when they support an executive message.
• The final takeaways are strategic and do not simply repeat previous sections.
• The report is suitable for Directors, Product, Operations, Risk, Technology, and Business leaders.

If any category scores below 9.5 out of 10, revise the report until every category reaches at least 9.5.

Do not show:

• Intermediate versions
• Internal scores
• Review comments
• Drafts
• Self-corrections

Only present the final improved version.
